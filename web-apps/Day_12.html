Day 12

Created Thursday 05 March 2020

He has graded our papers. Lots of people did very poorly. Only four people got above a 75. He states that a lot of people copied wrong previous answers.
He's spending the first bit of class lecturing us to be sure to actually pay attention and read the things he tells us to read.

Ticker - answers copied from previous exams.

Review

Q1) He didn't tab the recipe. That's why it was illegal.

Q2) It was an arithmetic overflow. (This is a common ticker answer he says)

Q3) The therac report was important because bad software kills people!. Two detrimental practices were poor documentation and refusal to listen to user reports (using old software is not a detrimental practice btw). However, they did use old software without properly testing it!

Q4) What is the function of the little old lady? There are various tasks that are well known to be needed in a programming group. She's the one who makes sure all the nasty little details are taken care of (little old lady comes in all genders, shapes, and sizes. Like a ripped body builder dude. Makes sure everything is the way it should be). One reason we refer to her as a 'little old lady' for in the past they were in fact a women who, for many years, worked at McGraw Publications who knew all the special little style techniques. She was very important due to this specialized knowledge.

Q5) TALK TO THE END USER! Even in the IEEE documentation, it says to do this. They can tell you what should be in the reports, what the screen should look like, etc. You can turn technical specs and turn them into non-technical terms for these people. They know exactly what they need so listen to them. Start from there and you'll avoid a great deal of misery. Don't trust the higher ups. They have no idea of what is actually required. It's cynical but it's a matter of observation says Gross.

Q6) What is the advantage of recursion over iteration? Nobody asked what they are! Don't just say they are functions that call themselves! In fact, that's wrong. They call copies of themselves. What is the advantage of recursion over iteration? They can be just as fast as for/while loops and can be simpler to write for solving a problem. The answer that he prefers is that: it makes the code much easier to read.

Q7) What does svn add do? It places a file under the control of Subversion. That's it. Nothing else. It places the file under subversion control. These are his exact words. If you do a commit on that working directory, it moves a copy into the repository.

Q8) 

((xfunc 1 2 3) 4 5 6)

It's syntactically correct. (func args). Everything hinges on what xfunc returns. That's what it all hinges on. If xfunc returns a function, then it's okay. Otherwise, it doesn't work. Remember, there is only one operator in scheme and that is evaluate! Y'know, ()? That's it.

Q9) Yes. Different files in different working directorys can have different version numbers. I answered yes but I fumbled.

Q10) Remember, it's not accuracy that changes between single and double precision. Double is more precise as you can tell due to the extra numbers. That doesn't make it more accurate though.

Q11) He gave us a badly written make file he admits. If any of the dependencies are newer than the target, then execute the recipe. Otherwise, don't. (He says don't use the word update unless you defined it in context i.e. up to date. Say older or newer.)

Q12) BNF was invented to specify the syntax of Algol 60.

Q13) Java requires a virtual machine. Poor memory management is also true. No pointer arithmetic. Java uses references over pointers. Saying because java has no pointers isn't good.

Q14) Scheme program time. I'll attach an image for this one. (He does wonder why some of us ended at 0 instead of 1. I terminated at 0 but I forget if I ran through the exponent. I don't believe I did). I ended up getting full credit regardless. Check out page Day 11 to see my answer.
