We began by doing introductions.

There will be three tests where each one is multiple choice and short answer. About 20 questions based on lecture for the most part.

We have three projects total. Only the third one is group work. About 5 people per group. We'll have a big presentation at the end in which we attempt to sell the project to the class. Everyone is required to commit so many lines of good code. The third project tests communication and coding skills.

For each project, we just make whatever we want.

When we are comfortable with frontend, we'll have our first test and project.

In pair programming, one person directs the other. And then we switch.

There will be no final exam. Just a final presentation.

When you think of software, think of documentation and code. The program itself can be a part of a system.

You should always be able to explain what your application does in technical terms.

We have off the shelf software and custom specified software.

For good software, we have:

  • Performance
  • Functionality
  • Well tested
  • Portable
  • Scalable
  • Maintainable
  • Simple
  • Self documenting
  • Accessible

Software engineering is implementation. Computer Science is the theory.

The cost of building software is 60% for the developers and 40% for the testing. The actual cost will vary from product or company.

There is no best way to solve a problem. There is no one best way to develop software. You can use a large variety of languages, techniques, etc. And it can all be correct in their own ways.

Software is also known as business.

Fundamentals:

  • Software Specification: the goal of the software
  • Software Design: the actual code
  • Implementation & Testing:
  • Maintenance

Try not to miss class! Espescially on test days. You can email the professor

Open source software is more of a current day thing. For a time, ,Microsoft even believed open source was un-American and stuck to propriety software. Today, Microsoft realizes how important it is and is embracing it.

Android runs on a modified version of linux. Google realeased the OS for phones in 2008 and help to skyrocket its popularity.

Major companies have decided to heavily adopt open source.

Some companies have some portions of their software free but other bits locked behind a paywall.

OpenSSL is incredibly needed on the internet but only maintained by a few people relying off of donations. People are trying to find ways to solve this funding probem.

What are the benefits of Open Source software?

  • Groundwork is done (you don't have to start from scratch designing the software)
  • More manpower
  • Extend the life of the project and add more features
  • Business
  • Managed via version control
  • Less likely to have bugs due to so many people addressing them and lending a fresh pair of eyes
  • Accessible
